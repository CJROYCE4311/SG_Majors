# 1st Annual Masters Tournament at Sterling Grove CC

## Event Snapshot

- Event: 2026 Masters Invitational at Sterling Grove Country Club
- Weekend: Friday, April 10, 2026 through Masters Sunday, April 12, 2026
- Location: Sterling Grove Country Club
- Commissioner: Eric Weiss
- Assistant Commissioner: Paul Benga
- Field: 20 two-man teams split into 2 flights of 10 teams each
- Competition: Pick-a-Pro flight tournament plus Calcutta
- Event type: member-led, members-only men's invitational after the club cancelled its own Masters tournament

## Weekend Logistics

- Eric Weiss coordinated space in the Flex Room at the main clubhouse for everyone to gather on Friday night before the tournament.
- Friday night gathering time was 5:45 p.m.
- Appetizers were arranged for the Friday gathering and paid for by Jon Vrolyks.
- The original food plan included sliders, wings, and flatbread pizza.
- A Calcutta was run on Friday night with a $6,300 pot.
- All pick-a-pro selections were completed on Friday night.
- After the tournament, the group met again to watch the finish of the Masters Tournament and divide the Calcutta pot.
- On Sunday after the round, the Flex Room was the meetup location, with the bar open for the group.
- Because the club would not support the event with tee times, the members secured the 10 tee times needed for the tournament themselves.

## Official Format

- Day 1 format: 2-man shamble using 75% of handicap, with 6 drives required per player.
- Day 2 format: 2-man best ball using 90% of handicap.
- Flight scoring: 2-man net team score plus selected pro Round 3 and Round 4 scores.
- Flights: Tiger Flight and Rory Flight.
- Entry fee: $200 per two-man team for the flight competition.
- Flight purse: $2,000 per flight.
- Flight payouts: 1st $1,200, 2nd $500, 3rd $300.
- Pick-a-Pro format: each team selected one A player and one B player on Friday night.
- Top 10 players in the world were automatically placed in the A pool.
- Multiple teams were allowed to select the same pros.
- Only weekend pro scores counted.
- Calcutta auction: starts at $50 per team and increases in $25 increments until sold.
- Calcutta coverage: one Calcutta across both flights.
- Calcutta scoring: based on team golf scores only, not the added pro scores.
- Calcutta buyback rule: if you are not the highest bidder for your own team, you may buy back half of the winning bid.
- Calcutta payouts: 1st 50%, 2nd 25%, 3rd 15%, 4th 10%.

## Rules and Scoring

- USGA rules applied for the event.
- Day 1 placement rule: players place the ball within one club-length of the selected drive, not nearer the hole, and in the same condition.
- Day 1 shamble note: no strokes were given on par 3s.
- Ruling questions were to be addressed by Paul Benga and Eric Weiss.
- In case of confusion or doubt, players were instructed to play a second ball and have the ruling determined by the golf shop after the round.
- Tournament scores were not to be posted to GHIN.
- If a player was out of the hole, he was to pick up to maintain pace of play.
- Otherwise, everyone needed to putt out. Failure to do so could result in disqualification.
- Paper scorecards and Squabbit were both used for scoring.
- The paper scorecard was final.
- One team in each foursome kept the paper scorecard; the other team entered scores in Squabbit.
- Squabbit entry was mandatory. Failure to enter scores in Squabbit resulted in disqualification.
- Scorecards had to be signed by both the competitor and marker and turned in to Paul Benga or Eric Weiss immediately after the round.
- Squabbit was treated as the tournament source of truth for pairings and tee times.
- Sunday tee times were set after Saturday's results and could be updated late.
- Blue tees were used for everyone.
- Native grass areas were not played as a hazard. If a ball could not be found there, players were instructed to re-tee.
- Exception: the large area on hole 8 marked in red was played as a hazard.
- On Sunday, the right side of hole 9 was played as casual water because of flooding.

## Handicapping Basis

- Course setup: Sterling Grove blue tees, par 72, rating 70.5, slope 124.
- Course handicap formula in the workbook: Handicap Index x (124 / 113) + (70.5 - 72).
- Shamble allowance: 75% of course handicap.
- Best ball allowance: 90% of course handicap.
- Handicaps were locked the week of the event to prevent late sandbagging.
